Act 1

Scene 1

Scene Summary:
Caesar returns to Rome after a recent victory, however two tribunes named Flavius and Marullus are concerned about the sway Caesar has over the people of Rome. They argue with a cobbler and carpenter over this, and state that Caesar's growing influence is troubling. They remove decorations from Caesar's statues to try and diminish his appearance.

Character Analysis:
Both Flavius and Marullus express heavy concern over Caesar's growing power. This is evidenced through Flavius's line of "These growing feathers plucked from Caesarâ€™s wing Will make him fly an ordinary pitch (Lines 77-78)," which expresses a desire to tear down Caesar. Scene 2

Scene Summary:
Caesar, his wife Calpurnia, Mark Antony, Brutus, and Cassius attend a Roman festival. While the crowd is preparing to celebrate, a soothsayer warns Caesar to "beware the Ideas of March." Caesar dismisses the soothsayer, but this is foreshadowing for his coming assassination. After Caesar leaves, Cassius is left alone with Brutus and questions him about his feelings towards Caesar's rise to power. Brutus admits he is concerned. Cassius then flatters Brutus and says that Caesar is weak and doesn't deserve his god-like status, as evidenced by when he fell sick in Spain or needed rescuing from the Tiver river. Brutus isn't fully convinced, but the seeds of doubt are sown within him.
